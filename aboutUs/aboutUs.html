The Wainui & Waitoki Watch (WWW) has been in publication since May 2016. Our goal and aim has always been to provide a quality colour monthly newsletter which supports our local small businesses, our rural schools and community events. We are a non-profit publication of locally based mums and grandparents with enthusiasm and passion for our rural and lifestyle communities. We even had poll on Facebook for you to submit what we should call the publication and a local Waitoki mum came up with the fantastic name! We have decided to re-refresh and change the look of the publication and bring in some new ideas to benefit everyone. We love covering your stories and sharing ideas of improving our community spirit and connections. We enjoy rolling up our sleeves and rallying around to support our local schools with fund raising events and sponsorship. We look to you to keep us informed of upcoming events or if you are launching a new business or product. We are now putting the publication online just in case you are out of our catchment areas for the rural delivery posties, we will put a link every month on the local community Facebook pages which you can download and save. We are now increasing our reach to include our Dairy Flat kinsfolk as many have expressed they would like to be included and also want to support local businesses. We are launching a classified column where you can advertise your business for a very low cost and are guaranteed a monthly reach of 2000 homes. Should you prefer more substantial advertising we can offer a range of sizes which will help give your business more exposure. We are reaching out to you to support the Waitoki & Wainui Watch publication by helping us to be well informed of the services, goods and events you are offering. We would also like to hear your stories, recent or historic, so do not hesitate to take a few mins to share. Please contact our editor Charlotte Hopwood on her email: contact.the.newsletter.editor@gmail.com

In summary our intention is to provide local information that supports events and new initiatives in our area, we are excited about the refresh – we hope you will be too!

Thank you for your support. Looie James, Tracey Howe (pictured left to right) Charlotte Hopwood (Editor) and Melanie Mayes (Graphic Designer)
